Referee report

Article ID:A/255635/PAP
Title:On the location of the surface-attached globule phase in collapsing polymers.
 
First referee's report


Authors have analyzed the phase diagram of the configurational phases of a polymer chain with one end attached to the surface. The main focus seems to rely on distinction between figs 3(a) and 3(b), i.e., the surface attached globule (SAG) and fully detached globule (FDG) as a function of the interaction parameters ww and wp. They seem to have estimated the number of visited sites (scaled and its variance) on the wall by the chain for different values of these interactions. Conformation and density profiles of homo-polymer chains are extensively studied (e.g., work by Binder and co.). This analysis of the phase diagram is a good start; however, the work presented as it is appears a bit speculative (even with a relatively lengthy description). For example, authors have frequently described the conformations of the chain using such terminologies as collapsed, extended, surface attached globule, but no analysis of the radius of gyration (Rg) of the chain is presented to augment these claims. A more detailed analysis is highly desirable for the conformation of the chain as a function of interaction strengths (even for the nearest neighbor interaction they seem to have considered) and temperature to clearly show various conformational phases including liquid phase of the chain. Some of my specific remarks follow.

1. Page 4 ‘In [8, 9, 10] it was … the Desorbed-Extended phase. We shall refer to this second situation as the “Fully Detached Globule” ‘. Why it is globule if detached extended? Definition of globule with explicit scaling of Rg with its size will help clarifying this issue.
2. Page 5 ‘Let us consider the lines ww = 1.3 (attractive wall visits) and ww = 0.8 (repulsive wall visits).’ it would be good to point out the interaction strength and temperature explicitly.
3. page 5 ‘ Hence the (finite-size location) … phase).’, not clear from fig. 4.
4. In figure 5, is y-label ‘scaled visits’ or just the mean number of visits? Conformation of chain will depend on the polymer-wall interaction. ‘We conclude … wp = 0.8.’, please provide Rg profile to augment this conclusion. As mentioned above, it would be good to scaling of Rg with n to judge how the globule and extended conformations set in.
5. Just before the last sentence on page 6 ‘ While … correction exponent used to do the extrapolation.’ appears vague.
6. In figure 7, is wp = 1.0 attractive? Which one (7(a), 7(b)) to take ( = 0 or 2/3)?
7. Rg for corresponding figs. 8 and 9 should be presented.
8. Last sentence (page 8) ‘It is entirely possible however that much longer polymer lengths need to be considered before the surface phase diagram can be conclusively delineated.’ what is then the conclusion?
